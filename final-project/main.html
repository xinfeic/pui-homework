ABOUT ME

Apart from my curriculum, as a research assistant at CMU's LearnLab, my research interest lies in statistical pedagogy.

In addition, my internships provided me the opportunity to explore time series forecasting, clustering, and unsupervised learning methods across diverse domains, from telecommunications to physiological sciences.

Currently majoring in Statistics and Machine Learning with Human-Computer Interaction, I'm drawn to the intersection of data science, HCI and psychology for quantitative UX research, alongside the interdisciplinary study of cognitive neuroscience and AI to revolutionize personalization in design, offering experiences uniquely shaped by human-centered data science.

During my spare time, I love traveling to escape into the world. I've journeyed as far north as the Arctic Circle but yearned to explore the south of the equator. Skydiving, scuba diving, and always marveling at nature's wonders like auroras and geysers highlight my adventures. Beyond traveling, photography, racket sports, and doodling are my cherished activities for relaxing.
